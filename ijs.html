IJS Judging Interface


In the early 2000’s, figure skating changed its way of judging competitions after the 2002 Winter Olympics figure skating scandal, transitioning from the “pen-and-paper” 6.0 system to the digital International Judging System (IJS). With the introduction of a new structure came an interface that became more difficult for judges to understand and get used to.

Collaborating with the judges and coaches from the figure skating community, I am currently conducting research on:

  • The different functions of the IJS layout
  • How judges input their scores into the system
  • How the judging data is managed on the network
  • Which equipment is necessary for varying scopes of competitions
  • How data is sent between various roles of the technical panel and the judges
  • Which issues judges may have with the user interface

Prospectively, the interface prototype should be finished by end of Summer 2019.
