MindoroBots focused on low-cost data collection of coral reefs to aid its preservation. Current conservation efforts require divers to manually photograph strips of the coral reefs, which can be time consuming, costly, and limited. The team developed an economic, motorized, and remote-controlled swarm-robot that could stitch together PVC quadrats to form uniform large visuals of coral reef strips - these prototypes were tested on the remote Filipino island of Mindoro. The results of this project implicate we can aid conservation through automation.

As a business student on a HKU engineering team, I researched and wrote about the cost-effectiveness of the swarm-bot, economic factors of the Philippines as well as the feasibility of the project in the long-term. This project was an extremely valuable experience in how I was able to expand beyond my business degree by shadowing knowledgeable engineering students and how I received helpful guidance from the team's leadership. It is through this work that I developed a desire to learn about and contribute to the technological field.
