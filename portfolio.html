Processing Art


Recently, I had began my coding endeavours through Processing, a program with powerful visual arts capabilities. I made my artistical ideas come alive by combining various concepts from the book in order to develop my first digital art project. I realized this is one of the few tools that enable me to experiment with 3D art, one of my favorite categories which I have not been able to enjoy before. In time, I hope to expand my knowledge of Processing to create significant artworks as well as using the program as a means to transition into other programming languages.

Mindorobots


Mindorobots focused on low-cost data collection of coral reefs to aid its preservation. Current conservation efforts require divers to manually photograph strips of the coral reefs, which can be time consuming, costly, and limited. The team developed an economic, motorized, and remote-controlled swarm-robot that could stitch together PVC quadrats to form uniform large visuals of coral reef strips - these prototypes were tested on the Filipino island Mindoro. The results of this project implicate we can aid conservation through automation.

As a business student on a HKU engineering team, I researched and wrote about the cost-effectiveness of the swarm-bot, economic factors of the Philippines as well as the feasibility of the project in the long-term. This project was an extremely valuable experience in how I was able to expand beyond my business degree by shadowing knowledgable engineering students and how I received helpful guidance from the team's leadership. It is through this work that I developed a desire to learn about and contribute to the technological field.

The project was formed by Cesar Harada, Sidhant Gupta, and Rohak Singhal, and placed national runner-up for the James Dyson Award.
