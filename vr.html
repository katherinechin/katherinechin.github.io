VR Game


I developed a virtual reality game to promote children’s interest in VR and contribute to rehabilitation for the elderly. In this kid-friendly game, the goal is to prevent spawning penguins from leaving their icy home into the vast ocean. The player can point and launch snowballs at the penguins to push them backward as they continue moving towards the water; snowballs can be launched from either hands by merely pressing the trigger buttons.

This project was inspired by Boston’s Museum of Science Particle Mirror exhibit, an Xbox Kinect simulation where the audience interacts with various special effects from a video feed. Using Unity 3D, I learned how to code in C# on my own in order to publish this game to the Oculus Store.

Currently, I am in contact with the Boston Children’s Museum on creating a VR exhibit using this game to inspire curiosity to kids on the capabilities of VR and spark their interest in technology as a creative pursuit. Additionally, I am providing this VR experience to elderly homes to promote mobility, hand-eye coordination, alertness, and enjoyment for sedentary residents.
